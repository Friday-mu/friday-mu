# Multilingual & Cultural Communication

## Language Matching

Respond in the guest's apparent preferred language. Detection cues (priority order):
1. Language of guest's most recent substantive message in the thread
2. Language of prior team-sent messages to this guest
3. Channel/booking metadata if it captures language preference
4. Name-based heuristic (French/Spanish/Portuguese/Russian/etc. name cues)

Default to English only when no other signal indicates otherwise.

## French (21% of visitors)

- Formal address (vous), detailed responses
- 30% higher satisfaction vs English-only
- Key phrases:
  - Welcome: "Bienvenue à votre propriété de vacances"
  - Emergency: "En cas d'urgence, appelez le 999"
  - Checkout: "Départ avant 10h00, merci"
  - WiFi: "Réseau WiFi: [nom] Mot de passe: [mot de passe]"
  - Contact: "Contactez-nous: WhatsApp +230 [numéro]"
- Preferences: Later dining (8-9 PM), wine knowledge, sophisticated cultural recommendations
- Response pattern: Prefer comprehensive responses vs brief acknowledgments
- Tips: Appreciate local Mauritian-French cultural connections; fine dining and art recommendations valued

## German

- Direct, precise, information-rich
- Detailed guides and transport info
- Preferences: Punctuality emphasis, efficiency, nature/hiking recommendations
- Booking behavior: Research-heavy, appreciate comprehensive pre-arrival information
- Tips: Sustainability credentials matter; provide detailed maps

## British

- Polite, appreciates subtle humor, traditional hospitality
- Preferences: Beach-centric, sun/shade balance, familiar food options alongside local cuisine
- Service style: Proactive but non-intrusive, respect for privacy
- Tips: Currency guidance helpful; tea/coffee facilities important; weather-focused seasonal content

## Indian (+33% growth — emerging priority)

- Respectful, family-oriented, visual aids helpful
- Preferences: Vegetarian options critical, multi-generational travel needs, religious considerations
- Cultural note: "Guest as god" cultural expectation = elevated service standards
- Tips: Dietary restrictions understanding essential; prayer space awareness; family group accommodation needs

## South African

- Informal English, practical focus
- Preferences: Outdoor adventures, water sports, value-for-money emphasis
- Advantage: Same timezone enables real-time communication
- Tips: Currency exchange guidance; similar cultural understanding; budget-conscious messaging

## Best Practices

- Send key documents (check-in instructions, emergency info, house rules) in guest's language
- Use professional translation for legal and safety documents, AI translation for casual messages
- Include visual aids (icons, photos, diagrams) to supplement text for language barriers
- QR codes linking to translated versions of property guides
- Kreol phrases as cultural touch points enhance authenticity
