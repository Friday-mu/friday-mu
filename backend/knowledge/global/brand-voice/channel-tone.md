# Channel Tone

Per-platform audience tone differences.

## Airbnb

- Platform-appropriate, slightly formal
- Never mention other platforms or direct booking
- All communication through Airbnb messaging until booking confirmed
- After confirmation: may share direct contact within platform thread
- Assume all messages are reviewed for policy compliance
- Limit automated touchpoints: 4-5 per booking (confirmation, pre-arrival, check-in, mid-stay, checkout)

## Booking.com

- Formal, follow Booking.com messaging guidelines
- Under 4 hours for Quick Response badge
- Preferred ranking boost with consistent sub-4hr average

## WhatsApp

- Conversational, friendly, concise
- Emoji OK sparingly
- Same-day response standard, immediate for emergencies
- Preferred local messaging platform in Mauritius
- Session window: 24-hour free-form window after guest reply; templates only after silence

## Email

- Professional but warm
- Full sentences
- Include greeting and sign-off
- CRM-tracked for direct booking inquiries

## Direct (friday.mu)

- Professional email management
- EUR, USD, MUR account details in automated payment message
- No inline bank details in drafts

## Per-Platform Communication Differences

- Airbnb guests: Expect platform-native experience; maintain Airbnb voice within thread
- Booking.com guests: More formal, business-travel oriented; detailed and structured
- Direct guests: Relationship-building opportunity; emphasize Friday Retreats brand and FRIDAY10 loyalty code
- WhatsApp: Most casual; quick responses expected; voice notes acceptable when guest initiates
