# Complaint Escalation

## 4E Model

Empathise → Explain → Execute → Elevate. Never be defensive.

## L1: Friendly & Welcoming

Standard communications, happy guests, routine inquiries.

- Tone: Warm, enthusiastic, personal
- Example: "We're so excited to host you! Here's a little insider tip for your first evening..."

## L2: Concerned & Attentive

Minor issues, mild dissatisfaction, maintenance requests.

- Tone: Empathetic, solution-focused, reassuring
- Example: "I'm sorry to hear about that. I've already contacted our maintenance team and they'll be there within the hour. In the meantime, here's what you can do..."
- Actions: Apologize immediately, provide honest ETA with buffer, follow up 2-4 hours after fix

## L3: Firm & Professional

Repeated issues, house rule violations, unreasonable demands, noise complaints.

- Tone: Direct but respectful, clear boundaries, documented
- Example: "I understand your perspective and I want to find a solution that works. However, our house rules clearly state... I'd appreciate your cooperation so we can ensure a great experience for everyone."
- Actions: Reference specific rules, document all communication, offer resolution within policy

## L4: Final & Documented

Serious violations, safety concerns, potential eviction, legal matters.

- Tone: Formal, factual, legally protective
- Example: "This is a formal notice regarding [specific violation]. As outlined in our rental agreement, [specific clause]. We require immediate compliance. If this continues, we will need to [specific consequence]. This communication is being documented."
- Actions: Document everything, involve management, cease non-essential communication

---

## Complaint Handling Rules

1. Lead with empathy before solutions
2. Give honest ETAs with buffer — under-promise, over-deliver
3. Always close the loop: follow up 2-4 hours after fix
4. Document all communications for potential platform disputes
