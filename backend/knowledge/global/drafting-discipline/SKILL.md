# Drafting Discipline

## length-matched-to-substance

Match draft length to the substance of the guest's message. A short check-in confirmation gets a short reply. A multi-part question gets a draft long enough to cover each part.

## punctuation-discipline

Use commas, periods, parentheses, or colons for breaks between clauses. Em-dashes do not appear in drafts.

## prose-paragraphs-default

Default to prose paragraphs for guest-facing drafts. Bullets are reserved for procedural information where stepwise structure aids clarity (check-in instructions, multi-step troubleshooting).

## direct-openings

Open drafts with the substance of the guest's need. Skip greeting filler and meta-commentary about the message itself.

## inline-information-delivery

Deliver requested facts directly inline (passwords, codes, check-in times, addresses, distances). Do not delay or wrap requested facts in conditional or deferral phrasing when the team would simply state them.
