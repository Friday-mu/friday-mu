# Learning, Controls, And Agent Safety

## What Friday Consult Should Learn

- Actual duration patterns by task template, property, size, staff member, and season.
- Repeated missing requirements for a property.
- Staff skill improvements or restrictions.
- Vendor performance and typical lead times.
- Travel-time reality by route and time of day.
- Repeated guest issue patterns that should become preventative work.

## What Requires Human Review Before Learning

- Large duration deltas.
- Staff mistakes or sensitive performance notes.
- Anything that changes owner-charge policy.
- Anything that changes legal, payment, tax, or contractual interpretation.
- Property facts that conflict with Guesty/property cards.
- Vendor price changes not backed by quote/invoice.

## Confidence Gates

- High confidence: exact data present; action is reversible or low-risk; no owner approval needed.
- Medium confidence: data mostly present but there is uncertainty in duration, travel, staff availability, or scope. Draft, ask for confirmation.
- Low confidence: missing property, no owner approval state, unclear urgency, unclear staff availability, or cost beyond limits. Ask for missing info and avoid applying.

## Reversible Vs Approval-Routed Actions

Reversible/low-risk:

- Draft a schedule.
- Clear visible times.
- Undo latest schedule operation.
- Draft a task from a reported issue.
- Add a reminder to verify something.

Approval-routed/high-risk:

- Owner-chargeable expenses above routine limit.
- Urgent override use.
- Major repairs/upgrades.
- Final guest confirmation when payment/funds are unclear.
- Any action affecting confirmed reservations, owner payouts, or legal obligations.

## Audit Expectations

Every applied action should record:

- who requested it
- what changed
- why
- source evidence
- confidence level
- whether owner/staff approval was required
- before/after state for undo where feasible

## Human Interface Pattern

Friday Consult should behave like an operator partner:

1. Discuss and explain.
2. Draft a plan.
3. Let the human edit.
4. Apply only explicit approved changes.
5. Keep undo available.
6. Learn only after verification.
